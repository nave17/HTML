The Band Neffex


General Information

Bryce Savage and Cameron Wales are an American music duo. They make remixes and original tracks that are a hybrid of electronic and rap music. Wales used to compose the instrumental and edit the song, while Savage writes the words and sings. Songs "Fight Back" and "Grateful" have more than 100 million views, among the most popular music videos.


Career Information

Savage and Wales first met in high school when they were 15 years old. In 2017, the duo set up a challenge for releasing 100 songs in 100 weeks.
On October 16, 2019, they released their first EP titled Q203, named after the apartment where they recorded their first songs. The album has six songs: When I Was Young, Without You, It's My Life, Sunday, Primal, and Want Me. On September 25, 2020, they released their debut album titled "New Beginnings" featuring ROZES, Jez Dior, and MASN. On July 6, 2021, they mutually decided to part ways and each one focus on solo projects, leaving Savage left in NEFFEX.

100 songs in 100 weeks/Music Popularity

NEFFEX decided to put themselves to the test and polish their abilities by releasing a whole song every week for the next 100 weeks.
Inspired by musicians like Lil Wayne and Russ, who were releasing complete albums and songs on a regular basis online.
Neffex posted a song every Wednesday at 9 a.m. to its social media accounts, including YouTube and streaming platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, and Soundcloud.
In under two years, the self-released, mixed, and produced tracks "Fight Back," "Rumors," "Careless," "Life," "Soldier," and "Best of Me" amassed almost 2 million YouTube subscribers and over a billion cumulative streams.
