Darwin

Welcome to Darwin, the leisurely capital of the Northern Territory of Australia, with its warm nights, colourful inhabitants, and outdoor experiences. Along with markets, crocodiles, and cruises, the city is home to significant amazingÂ cultures, as shown by the diverse languages spoken on the streets and the vivid Aboriginal art that fills the city's galleries, museums, and streetscapes. Because of the city's closeness to Asia, considerable Asian influences may be found particularly in the vibrant culinary scene and bustling night markets.

Darwin's Food

Darwin is a modern culinary pleasure thanks to its closeness to Asia and ocean of fresh seafood. Savour the flavours at some of Darwin's best dining establishments. Local products including as mud crab, barramundi, Kakadu plum, lemon myrtle, and even green ants are used in the cuisine. Don't worry, the green ants taste much better than they sound. When it comes to dining in Darwin, there are lots of alternatives.

Great Barrier Reef

"THE GREAT BARRIER REEF IS ONE OF THOSE PLACES YOU MUST VISIT, WHETHER YOU LOVE THE WATER OR JUST WANT TO SEE ONE OF THE GREAT WONDERS OF THE WORLD."

-Chris Hemsworth, actor and Global Ambassador for Tourism Australia


The Great Barrier Reef was the first coral reef system to be designated as a World Heritage Site, having met all four natural criteria for inclusion. The reef is the biggest living structure on the planet, and it can even be viewed from space! With over 100 lovely islands strewn over the reef to explore, it is one of Australia's most popular attractions. With rainbow-colored corals and a diversity of marine creatures to observe up close, the snorkelling and diving possibilities are among the greatest in the world. The region is also culturally significant to the local Aboriginal people, who have handed down legends about the reef's formation down the years.

Things to do and attractions at the Great Barrier Reef

Byron Bay

Byron Bay was a peaceful little industrial town with a little stench of meat in the air thanks to the local slaughterhouse and whaling station half a century ago. Today, Australia's most easterly point in northern New South Wales is a popular tourist attraction, with over 2 million people each year.

The Atmosphere

Byron Bay is known for its distinct atmosphere, which is an important element of the "Byron" experience. Many local hippies and street artists can be seen all around town, and there is usually something going on. Walking through the streets, you'll notice little colourful stores and dining establishments that reflect Byron Bay's unconventional lifestyle. When you combine the incredibly easygoing environment, the Byron Bay people, the numerous pubs and clubs, and the thriving live music scene, it's difficult not to have a good time in Bryon Bay!
